The Art of Henna

A paste made from the powdered leaves of the henna plant is made to release the plant's dying qualities. The paste is then applied to the body to create intricate semi-permanent designs called Mehndi, initially leaving a vibrate orange hue on the skin which matures into a much deeper reddish-brown hue with time.

Dating back to ancient India, mehndi is still a popular form of body art among the women of the Indian subcontinent, Africa and the Middle East - most recognisable for its use in wedding rituals. However, the versatility Mehdi can be applied in has led to a larger attraction from western cultures, as a bold, temporary fashion statement.

About the Artist

Kaya Patel is a henna artist based in South London. Having been working with henna since a young age, Kaya has extensive experience in both traditional and contempory designs, working with each client to make unique pieces of art.

"Having been lucky enough to be exposed to henna at a young age through family functions and wedding, I started practicing patterns and shapes whenever I got the chance. After recieiving numbermous compliments about the doodles on my hands, I realised that something I consdiered as a hobby could become a business, and thus Henna by Kaya was born." - Kaya Patel
